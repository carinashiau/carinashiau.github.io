## About
Hi there! My name is Carina. I'm a third-year medical student at Harvard with an interest in computational genomics and cutaneous oncology.

Previously, I studied biology at Cornell University, where I wrote an honors thesis on protein modeling with the Ovchinnikov Lab. Prior to medical school, I worked as an associate computational biologist at the Hwang Lab at the Broad Institute and the Massachusetts General Hospital, where I developed computational frameworks to better understand the spatial molecular landscape of untreated and treated pancreatic cancer. More recently, I've been focused on applying single-cell technologies to cutaneous T-cell lymphoma at the Clark Lab.

Feel free to reach out if you’d like to connect!
